UX MetroCard Project


Since the 1990â€™s, the most recent version of the metro card has been easily susceptible to being bent, lost, and almost impossible to scan correctly the first time. In addition to the detrimental alterations that can be made physically to the MetroCard, there is also the virtual component. When users are unsure of whether they have enough money on their metro card for a full fair or not, they can either swipe their card at the fare box or at the turnstile. If the user verifies that the current amount of money on their metro card is not enough, they must then turn to another machine to increase the value on their metro card, which takes fixed amounts of money. More specifically there are multiple machines made for the user to add value to their MetroCard, some accept bills and the others accept a credit or debit card.

My overall objective is eliminating the time it takes to purchase an MTA MetroCard. The problem I am solving is the user missing his or her train due to being on a time crunch. My app Transit Mobile allows the user to spend extra time wrapping up a presentation for work, notes for a test, or errands around the house, without worrying about missing the train due to complications derived from the MetroCard. My application of a virtual MetroCard will allow the user to push the margins of getting from one destination to another, as well as fluid navigation amongst tight deadlines.
